Summary of the novel. Themes and survival.

Life of Pi

Life of Pi follows a vegetarian teenage boy named Pi who becomes stranded on a lifeboat in the Pacific Ocean with a tiger named Richard Parker. Much of the novel focuses on Pi’s struggle to survive, both physically and mentally, as he learns to coexist with the tiger. While it is a survival story on the surface, the novel also explores themes of spirituality, storytelling, and how people interpret traumatic experiences. By presenting more than one possible version of the events, the story encourages readers to question what truth really means and consider which version of the story is more comfortable to accept.
